How it works

From a name to a dossier, in three steps.

No jargon, no guesswork. Give us what you have and get back a report you can act on.

1

Search a dentist

Name and state, or a license number — whatever you have. No account, no forms.

2

We pull the records

Live license and board standing, disciplinary filings, court records, and reviews — assembled into one dossier.

3

Get the dossier

A single risk read: what's flagged, why, and the source behind every negative finding.

🦷 License & board record

Real, current license standing.

Straight from the state board's own roster — active, delinquent, or restricted — with issue and expiration dates. Not a stale screenshot; the real record.

  • Live status pulled from official board data
  • Issue, expiration, and secondary status
  • 43k+ California dentists covered today
🚩 Disciplinary actions

Accusations and orders, shown as the record states them.

Board accusations, citations, and decisions — presented as allegations with their status, never as verdicts. Each one attributed and dated.

  • Pending vs. resolved, clearly marked
  • Every action linked to its source document
  • Public-record framing — no editorializing
⚖️ Litigation

Malpractice and wrongful-death cases, surfaced.

Court records where the provider is a named defendant, classified by nature, so a genuine malpractice case doesn't hide in a pile of unrelated filings.

  • Malpractice and wrongful-death flagged
  • Provider-as-defendant, not just any mention
  • Case caption, court, and status with a source link
⭐ Reviews, weighted

A rating you can actually trust.

Reviews and discipline are different signals, so we never blend them. Confidence is driven by the verified sample only — a big unverified headline can't inflate it.

  • Verified vs. unverified sources, side by side
  • Confidence label on every aggregate
  • Reviews never override the record
🔎 Provenance

No verdict without a resolvable citation.

Every negative finding must trace to a verbatim quote in a source we fetched. Anything that can't be sourced is quarantined before you ever see it. That's the whole point.

  • Verbatim quote behind each adverse claim
  • Unsourced findings are dropped, not shown
  • Built to survive a legal challenge
